Canberra's Modern Architecture

Exploring the city's significant mid and late twentieth-century buildings. Some face threats from demolition or inappropriate alterations — your advocacy can make a difference.

At Risk

Churchill House

  • 220 Northbourne Ave, Braddon
  • Built: 1969
  • Robin Boyd

Designed by prominent Australian architect Robin Boyd, Churchill House is considered one of the most important modernist buildings in Canberra. It currently faces threats of demolition and inappropriate alterations.

Find out more about the campaign to save it →
Heritage Listed

Callam Offices

  • Callam Street, Woden
  • Built: 1971
  • John Andrews

A striking example of brutalist government architecture by architect John Andrews. The bold geometric forms and exposed concrete reflect the confident modernism of Canberra's expansion during the 1970s.

Learn more →
At Risk

Civic Square

  • London Circuit, Canberra City
  • Built: 1963
  • Nominated Architect: Various

The civic heart of Canberra City, Civic Square is an important gathering space for the community. Its modernist paving and surrounding buildings create a distinctive urban character that is under ongoing development pressure.

Find out more →
At Risk

Colonial Mutual Building

  • City Walk, Canberra City
  • Built: 1968
  • Col & Owen & Bard, Rigpon

This building played a vital role in shaping Canberra's commercial centre in the post-war period. Its distinctive curtain wall facade is among the finest examples of commercial modernism in the ACT and faces redevelopment threats.

Learn more →
Heritage Listed

Torrens Town Houses and Shopping Complex

  • Masson Street, Torrens
  • Built: 1965

An innovative integration of residential and commercial use, the Torrens development was a pioneering example of mixed-use planning in Canberra's post-war suburban expansion.

Learn more →
Heritage Listed

ANZAC Park East and West (Postal Buildings)

  • Anzac Park, Reid/Campbell
  • Built: 1959
  • Commonwealth Department of Works

These matching office blocks on either side of Anzac Park are a defining element of Canberra's parliamentary triangle precinct, representing the ordered post-war vision for Australia's capital.

Learn more →
Heritage Listed

National Library of Australia

  • Parkes Place, Parkes
  • Built: 1968
  • Bunning and Madden

One of Canberra's most celebrated buildings, the National Library combines a classical colonnade with modernist detailing in travertine marble. It anchors the southern shore of Lake Burley Griffin.

Learn more →
Heritage Listed

University House

  • Balmain Crescent, Acton
  • Built: 1954
  • Brian Lewis

The first major building completed at the Australian National University, University House reflects the aspirations of post-war academic life in Australia. Its garden courtyards and modernist interiors are intact and significant.

Learn more →
At Risk

Giralang Primary School

  • Namatjira Drive, Giralang
  • Built: 1975
  • Smith Taggart

This innovative school design by architects Smith Taggart pioneered open-plan learning environments in Australia. The campus layout and timber construction were ahead of their time and remain largely intact.

Find out more about its future →
Heritage Listed

Australian Academy of Science: The Shine Dome

  • Gordon Street, Acton
  • Built: 1959
  • Roy Grounds

Roy Grounds' iconic flying-saucer dome is one of Australia's most recognisable buildings. The shell-concrete structure remains a masterpiece of engineering and modernist design, and is listed on the National Heritage List.

Innovative housing co-operatives showcasing progressive community-focused residential design. These developments demonstrate how mid-century planning principles created sustainable, people-centred neighbourhoods.
